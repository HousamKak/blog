The first edition of *The Housam & Review*. A collage of dispatches, fake classifieds, a horoscope for programmers, obituaries for Internet Explorer 6 and jQuery-in-new-projects, and a weather forecast that rates Thursdays as "unverified — pending peer review".

## What appears on the front page

- A lead dispatch with a drop-capped headline and paper-tape decorations
- An "at a glance" tally printed in typewriter
- A horoscope panel scribbled in the margins of the week
- A Goudy & Co. advertisement ("now with open bowls")
- Obituaries for the departed
- Classifieds in two columns of *Special Elite*
- Weather: partly ironic, speculative, unverified, deployed

## Provenance

The masthead is set in **Engravers' Old English** (the OPTI cut from 1990, after the 1911 Bitstream original). The ampersand in the title is from **Goudy Bookletter 1911** — a font that contains only one glyph, the `&`, designed specifically to be dropped into other families. The working clock to the left of the title uses SVG-rendered Roman numerals and updates on the second boundary.

The body is **Playfair Display** at weight 900 for headlines, **EB Garamond** for prose, **Cinzel** for small-caps metadata, **Special Elite** for typewriter notes, and **Homemade Apple** + **Permanent Marker** for the scribbles.
