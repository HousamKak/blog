## Overview

The paper is in two parts.

**Part I — The Uneven World.** A philosophical case that inequality at birth is historically produced (enclosure, colonial extraction, slavery, inherited property, capital accumulation) rather than a natural accident or the outcome of individual effort. It draws on Locke, Rousseau, Paine, George, Marx, Polanyi, Rawls, Nozick, Sen, Ostrom, Van Parijs, and Atkinson to argue that treating part of the social surplus of civilization as a common inheritance is a requirement of justice — not charity.

**Part II — The Mathematical Framework.** A formal proposal: the **Social Starting Capital Framework (SSCF)**. It combines:

- A universal child floor
- A family allocation rule with household taper
- A protected birthright capital account for each child
- An education-linked staged unlock mechanism
- A sovereign social wealth fund financed by resource rents, land value taxation, inheritance taxation, public asset profits, and ecological levies

## Design Constraints

The mathematical model is built to satisfy three constraints simultaneously:

1. Every child must possess an irreducible claim on society's inherited wealth.
2. Family decisions should matter without annihilating the child's claim.
3. The system must remain demographically and fiscally sustainable.

## What the Paper Provides

Definitions, formulas, examples, and basic propositions establishing non-zero child claims, bounded family expenditure growth, and a simple sustainability condition for the underlying fund. A toy calibration and a demographic shock example illustrate the mechanics. Comparative statics analyze the roles of the child protection parameter $\alpha$, the family-size sensitivity $\mu$, the household taper design $(K^*, \lambda)$, and the intergenerational tradeoff $(\phi, \gamma)$.

The result is a synthesis of political philosophy and formal policy design aimed at replacing the moral fiction of equal opportunity with an institutionally real starting stake for every person.
